Urgent Report: Mass Displacement Crisis in Gaza

The United Nations Relief and Works Agency for Palestine Refugees (UNRWA) announced that 1.9 million people have been forcibly displaced repeatedly in Gaza Strip, representing 90% of the total population of the Strip.

According to Human Rights Watch, the Israeli army has ordered around 400,000 people in northern Gaza to leave their homes since October 1st and prevented aid from reaching them, in a forced displacement operation that may amount to a war crime.

Repeated Displacement and Compounded Suffering

UN reports indicate that 9 out of every 10 people in Gaza have been displaced at least once , and in some cases displacement has been repeated 10 times for a single family, doubling the suffering of residents and increasing the difficulty of obtaining basic necessities.

Tragic Camp Conditions

Displaced persons live in harsh conditions inside overcrowded camps lacking:

  • Safe drinking water - Severe shortage threatening lives
  • Electricity - Near-complete power outages
  • Sanitation - Deteriorating health conditions
  • Adequate shelter - Tents and dilapidated buildings
  • Sufficient food - Worsening famine crisis

Historical Refugee Camps Under Bombardment

The eight historical camps in Gaza Strip, dating back to the mass displacement of Palestinians during the 1948 Nakba, have been subjected to intensive bombardment leading to widespread destruction and forcing their residents to be displaced once again.

These camps, which housed more than 1.7 million Palestinian refugees registered with UNRWA, have transformed from permanent shelters into danger zones, forcing their residents to search for new shelter amid ongoing military operations.

Humanitarian Aid Crisis

Humanitarian aid delivery operations face severe challenges, with deadly incidents recorded during aerial aid drops , while civilian gatherings around aid distribution centers face repeated targeting.

According to reports, more than 1,500 Palestinians were martyred and more than 10,000 others were injured by occupation forces' fire while attempting to obtain basic food and medical aid.

Impact of Displacement on Children and Women

Women and children constitute the largest proportion of displaced persons , facing additional difficulties in accessing healthcare and education. Displaced children suffer from severe psychological trauma as a result of losing their homes and being cut off from education for long periods.

International organizations warn that an entire generation of Palestinian children faces the risk of losing their right to education and normal development, threatening the future of Palestinian society as a whole.

The displacement crisis in Gaza represents one of the worst humanitarian disasters in modern history, where almost all residents need urgent humanitarian assistance, while the war continues to create more suffering and destruction daily.

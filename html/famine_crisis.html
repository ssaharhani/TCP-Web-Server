Breaking: Medical Report from Al-Aqsa Martyrs Hospital

The spokesperson for Al-Aqsa Martyrs Hospital, Dr. Khalil Al-Daqran, stated that the famine in Gaza Strip has entered stage five, confirming that health conditions are worsening amid a deliberate starvation policy pursued by the Israeli occupation, and with the absence of effective aid reaching the besieged citizens.

Al-Daqran pointed out, in an interview with Al Jazeera channel, that the number of malnutrition victims is continuously increasing, noting the registration of 175 deaths due to famine, including 6 cases within just 24 hours, alongside thousands of medical cases that hospitals receive daily.

Children suffering from severe malnutrition - the most affected demographic

He clarified that what the occupation promotes regarding the entry of food and medical aid is "completely false" , adding that what actually enters are limited quantities that do not meet even a small portion of the population's needs, and are often seized at crossings by lawless groups.

The spokesperson for Al-Aqsa Martyrs Hospital accused the American institution called "Gaza Humanitarian" of providing centers that have turned into "death traps" , saying that the occupation army regularly opens fire on civilians gathered near these centers, causing large numbers of wounded and martyrs.

Medical sources confirmed to Al Jazeera that 50 Palestinians were martyred since dawn on Sunday in scattered massacres, including 22 food seekers near aid control centers in Rafah and Nuseirat, where occupation forces have made it a habit to target civilian gatherings around them.

Hospital Occupancy Rate Doubles

Al-Daqran continued that the occupation's policy has caused a complete collapse of the health system , as bed occupancy rates in hospitals exceeded 300% , forcing medical staff to spread mattresses in corridors and courtyards to accommodate the influx of injured.

According to the spokesperson, hospitals are no longer able to receive more cases , due to the enormous number of patients, especially from the categories of children and elderly , who are the two groups most affected by malnutrition and the spread of chronic and infectious diseases.

He noted that children's departments in Gaza hospitals are witnessing unprecedented overcrowding , where doctors are forced to hospitalize 3 to 4 children on a single bed , amid a deteriorating health environment and widespread presence of rats and insects in displacement areas and camps.

Since the "Gaza Humanitarian" institution took control of aid flow in May, more than 1,500 Palestinians were martyred and more than 10,000 others were injured by fire from the occupation army and foreign contractors with the institution, according to the United Nations and the Ministry of Health in the stricken sector.

This escalation comes two days after the visit of American envoy Steve Witkoff to one of the aid centers in Rafah, at a time when observers see that such visits have become political cover for the continued killing of citizens under false humanitarian pretense.
